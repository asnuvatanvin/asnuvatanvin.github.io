Asnuva Tanvin

I obtained my B.Sc. in Computer Scinece & Engineering from Khulna University of Engineering & Technology(KUET), Khulna, Bangladesh in February 2023. I am currently working as a Lecturer in the department of CSE at United International University. My current research is experimenting with the effectiveness of transformer in forecasting multivariate time series and how to reduce parameters of the computationally expensive multihead self-attention. In addition to this, I am passionate about addressing challenges in the healthcare sector, both in Bangladesh and globally. I am eager to contribute to research in medical image and signal processing to develop robust technologies that can improve healthcare outcomes. I am also enthusiastic about research in sensor signal processing and mobile computing applications.

In my free time I love to do street photography and read books.

Research Experience

Effectiveness of Low Rank Matrix Factorization based Transformer for Time Series Forecasting

Supervisor: Dr. M.M.A. Hashem

Research Topic: Utilizing low rank matrix factorization strategy for vanilla transformers and perform experiments on multivariate time series data.

Tools: Pytorch, numpy, pandas, seaborn

  • Experimented with various types of implementation strategies of the Vanilla Transformer architecture for forecasting multiple future steps of multivariate time series.
  • Applied low rank factorization for approximating matrices of the attention layer and feed forward layer to reduce parameters and complexity of vanilla transformer.
  • Implemented the low rank matrix transformer on five different multivariate time series dataset to perform long range multi step forecasting and obtained noticeably good results.
  • One manuscript is under preparation for this research.

Undergraduate Thesis

Supervisor: Dr. M.M.A. Hashem

Research Topic: A Study on Forecasting Based on Spatio-Temporal Data Using Deep Learning Models

Tools: Keras, Tensorflow, Seaborn, Matplotlib

Code: Github

  • Studied characteristics of different types of time series data.
  • Experimented with various deep learning based techniques such as CNN, RNN, LSTM, GRU, BiLSTM, self-attention to replace statistical methods(ARIMA, SARIMA etc) for solving multivariate time series forecasting problem.
  • Applied additive decomposition as preprocessing technique to separate the trend, seasonality and residual components from time series and then developed deep learning based modules to forecast multiple future steps of each time series.
  • Implemented the architecture on three distinct multivariate time series, predicted multiple future horizons and obtained good results on each of them.
  • One paper has been published on this research.
